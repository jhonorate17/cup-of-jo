Blender Course Practice: Series I

This is the first in a series of Blender exercises that will track my progress as I learn more advanced techniques. Moonlight served as the reference for this project, and I recommend watching it if you haven’t already.

This project is my first exploration in Blender, completed as part of an independent study following a beginner-to-pro course. It is a recreation of a scene from the film, created to practice foundational tools. For this project, I focused exclusively on scale, rotate, and move operations, intentionally avoiding face editing or shape modeling to strengthen my understanding of spatial relationships and scene composition.

The project involved building the scene from scratch, arranging objects, and organizing space to match the cinematic reference. This exercise helped me establish a workflow and gain confidence in basic modeling and scene setup, providing a foundation for future projects.
